Prep: 10 minuntes || Cook: 40 minutes || Serves: 4

This recipe will definetely leave you wanting for more.

Ingredients:

Method:












Step 1

Soak white chickpeas in water overnight or for around 8-10 hours. Tie 1 teaspoon tea leaves in a plain muslin cloth.

Step 2

Pressure cook soaked white chickpeas with a tied tea leaves pouch, enough water and salt in a 3-liter capacity steel/aluminum pressure cooker over medium flame. After 4-5 whistles, turn off the flame and let the pressure comes down naturally. Open the lid and remove tied tea leaves pouch. Drain the excess water from boiled chickpeas in a large bowl. Keep the drained water to use later in the recipe.

Step 3

Dry roast bay leaf, black cardamom, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, dry red chilli, cloves and cinnamon in a pan until nice aroma starts or for around 1-minute over low flame. Let them cool for a while and then grind them in a small jar of a mixer grinder to make smooth dry punjabi masala powder.

Step 4

Mash 2 tablespoons boiled chickpeas with backside of a large spoon or grind in a grinder to make a medium coarse paste. Grind tomatoes in a mixer grinder to make tomato puree.

Step 5

Heat 3-tablespoons cooking oil in a kadai or pan over medium flame. Add chopped onion and sauté until it turns light brown or for approx. 1-2 minutes. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilli. Mix well and cook for 30 seconds.

Step 6

Add tomato puree and salt. Cook on medium flame until oil starts to separate, around 4-5 minutes. Stir it in between occasionally to prevent sticking. Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder and dry masala powder. Mix well and sauté for a minute.

Step 6

Add boiled chickpeas, mashed chickpeas and 1-cup water (preserved drained water of boiled chickpeas), mix well. Cook until gravy becomes thick, it will take around 4-5 minutes.

Step 6

Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve it hot with bhatura or steamed rice.
